Chapter 2

Earthmoving Materials and Operations

Swell
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Swell (%)}\\\\=\left(\,\frac{\textsf{Weight/bank volume}}{\textsf{Weight/loose volume}}-1\,\right)\times 100 }$$

Shrinkage
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Shrinkage (%)}\\\\=\left(\,1-\frac{\textsf{Weight/bank volume}}{\textsf{Weight/compacted volume}}\,\right)\times 100 }$$

Load Factor
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Load factor}=\frac{\textsf{Weight/loose unit volume}}{\textsf{Weight/bank unit volume}} }$$

Shrinkage Factor
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Shrinkage factor}\\\\=\frac{\textsf{Weight/bank unit volume}}{\textsf{Weight/compacted unit volume}} }$$

Triangular Spoil Bank
$$\displaylines{ B=\left(\,\frac{4V}{L\times\tan R}\,\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}\\\\ H=\frac{B\times\tan R}{2}\\\\ B=\textsf{base width}\\ H=\textsf{pile height}\\ L=\textsf{pile length}\\ R=\textsf{angle of repose}\\ V=\textsf{pile volume} }$$

Conical Spoil Pile
$$\displaylines{ D=\left(\,\frac{7.64V}{\tan R}\,\right)^{\frac{1}{3}}\\\\ H=\frac{D}{2}\times\tan R\\\\ D=\textsf{diameter of the pile base} }$$

Pit Excavations
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Volume}=\textsf{Horizontal area}\times\textsf{Average depth} }$$

Large Areas
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Weights}\\ \textsf{1 for corner points}\\ \textsf{2 for border points}\\ \textsf{4 for interior points}\\\\ \textsf{Average depth}=\frac{\textsf{Sum (Depth \(\times\) Weight)}}{\textsf{Sum of weights}} }$$

Chapter 3

Excavating and Lifting

Hydraulic Excavtors and Shovels
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Production Estimating}\\\\ \textsf{Production}=C\times S\times V\times B\times E\\\\ C=\textsf{cycles/h}\\ S=\textsf{swing-depth factor}\\ V=\textsf{heaped bucket volume}\\ B=\textsf{bucket fill factor}\\ E=\textsf{job efficiency} }$$

Draglines
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Estimated production}=\textsf{Ideal output}\\\times\,\textsf{Swing-depth factor}\times\textsf{Efficiency} }$$

Clamshells
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Production}\\=\textsf{Volume per cycle}\times\textsf{Cycles per hour} }$$

Chapter 4

Loading and Hauling

Notes
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Rolling resistance factor (lb/ton)}\\ =40+(\,30\times \textsf{in (penetration)}\,)\\\\ \textsf{Rolling resistance}\\=\textsf{Rolling resistance factor (lb/ton)}\\\times\,\textsf{Vehicle weight (ton)}\\\\ \textsf{Grade resistance factor (lb/ton)}\\ =20\times\textsf{grade (%)}\\\\ \textsf{Grade resistance}\\=\textsf{Grade resistance factor (lb/ton)}\\\times\,\textsf{Vehicle weight (ton)}\\\\ \textsf{Total resistance}=\textsf{Rolling resistance}\\ +\,\textsf{Grade resistance}\\\\ \textsf{Effective grade}=\textsf{Grade (%)}\\+\,\frac{\textsf{Rolling resistance factor (lb/ton)}}{20} }$$

Effect of Altitude
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Derating factor (%)}\\\\=3\times\left[\,\frac{\textsf{Altitude (ft)}-3000}{1000}\,\right]\\\\ \textsf{Percent rated power available}\\=100-\textsf{Derating factor} }$$

Effect of Traction
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Maximum available power}\\=\textsf{Maximum rimpull}\times\textsf{Rated power available}\\\\ \textsf{Maximum usable pull}\\=\textsf{Coefficient of traction}\times\textsf{Weight on drivers} }$$

Dozers
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Dozing time}=\frac{\textsf{Distance}}{\textsf{Dozing speed}}\\\\ 1\,mi/h=88\,ft/min\\\\ \textsf{Cycle time}=\textsf{Fixed time}+\textsf{Dozing time}\\+\,\textsf{Return time}\\\\ \textsf{Production}=\textsf{Volume per cycle}\times\textsf{Job efficiency} }$$

Loaders
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Cycle time}\\=\textsf{Basic loader cycle time}+\textsf{Travel time} }$$

Scrapers
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Fixed cycle time}=\textsf{Spot time}\\+\,\,\textsf{Load time}+\textsf{Maneuver and dump time}\\\\ \textsf{Effective grade}=\textsf{Grade (%)}\\+\,\frac{\textsf{Rolling resistance factor (lb/ton)}}{20}\\\\ \textsf{Variable cycle time}=\textsf{Haul time}\\ +\,\,\textsf{Return time}+\textsf{Turnaround time}\\\\ \textsf{Total cycle time}\\=\textsf{Fixed cycle time}+\textsf{Variable cycle time}\\\\ \textsf{Estimated production}\\=\textsf{Volume per cycle}\times\textsf{Job efficiency} }$$

Calculating the Number of Pushers Required
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Number of scrapers served}\\ =\frac{\textsf{Scraper cycle time}}{\textsf{Pusher cycle time}}\\\\ \textsf{Number of pushers required}\\ =\frac{\textsf{Number of scrapers}}{\textsf{Number served by one pusher}}\\\\ \textsf{Production}=\frac{\textsf{No. of pushers}}{\textsf{Required number}}\\\times\,\,\textsf{No. of scrapers}\times\textsf{Production per scraper} }$$

Determining the Number of Haul Units Needed
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Load time}\\=\frac{\textsf{Truck capacity}}{\textsf{Shovel production at 100% efficiency}}\\\\ \textsf{Number of trucks required}=\frac{\textsf{Truck cycle time}}{\textsf{Load time}}\\\\ \textsf{Expected production}\\=\textsf{Shovel production at 100% efficiency}\\\times\,\textsf{Job efficiency}\\\\ \textsf{Expected production}\\=\frac{\textsf{Actual number of units}}{N}\\\times\,\textsf{Excavator production} }$$
