Chapter - 5

Water Demand and Quantity

Arithmetical Increase Method
$$\displaylines{ P_n=P+n\,I\\ P_n=\textsf{future population at the end of n decades}\\ P=\textsf{present population}\\ I=\textsf{average increasement for a decade} }$$

Geometrical Increase Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Uniform Percentage Growth Method}\\\\ P_n=P\,\left(1+\frac{I_g}{100}\right)^n=P\,(\,1+r_g\,)^n\\\\ \textsf{(i) By arithmetic average method}\\ r_g=\frac{r_1+r_2+r_3+...+r_n}{n}\\\\ \textsf{(ii) By geometric average method}\\ r_g=(\,r_1\times r_2\times ...\times r_n\,)^{1/n}\\ I_g=(\,I_{g_1}\times I_{g_2}\times ...\times I_{g_n}\,)^{1/n}\\\\ I_g=\textsf{average percentage increase per decade}\\ r_g=\textsf{the increase per decade expressed as ratio} }$$

Incremental Increase Method
$$\displaylines{ P_n=P+n\,I+\frac{n\,(\,n+1\,)\,r}{2}\\\\ r=\textsf{average incremental increase} }$$

Decreased Rate of Growth Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Logistic Method}\\\\ P=\frac{P_s}{1+e^{a+b\,t}}\\\\ P_s=\frac{2\,P_0\,P_1\,P_2-P_1^2\,(\,P_0+P_2\,)}{P_0\,P_2-P_1^2}\\\\ a=\log_e\frac{P_s-P_0}{P_0}\\\\ b=\frac{1}{n}\,\log_e\frac{P_0\,(\,P_s-P_1\,)}{P_1\,(\,P_s-P_0\,)}\\\\ P_s=\textsf{saturation population}\\ P_0=\textsf{population at starting point A}\\ P=\textsf{population at any time t from origin A}\\ n=\textsf{the time interval between}\\\textsf{successive censuses} }$$

Determinatioin of Population for Inter-Censal and Post-Censal

Arithmetical Increase Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{For inter-censal period},\\ P_M=P_E+\frac{T_M-T_E}{T_L-T_E}\,(\,P_L-P_E\,)\\\\ \textsf{For post-censal period},\\ P_M=P_L+\frac{T_M-T_L}{T_L-T_E}\,(\,P_L-P_E\,) }$$

Geometrical Increase Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{For inter-censal period},\\ \log_{10}P_M=\log_{10}P_E\\+\,\frac{T_M-T_E}{T_L-T_E}\,(\,\log_{10}P_L-\log_{10}P_E\,)\\\\ \textsf{For post-censal period},\\ \log_{10}P_M=\log_{10}P_L\\+\,\frac{T_M-T_L}{T_L-T_E}\,(\,\log_{10}P_L-\log_{10}P_E\,) }$$

Chapter - 8

Screening and Aeration

Rate of Gas Absorption
$$\displaylines{ C_t-C_0=(\,C_s-C_0\,)+(\,C_t-C_s\,)\\\\ C_t-C_0=(\,C_s-C_0\,)\,[\,1-e^{(\,-K_g\,t\,)}\,]\\\\ \textsf{For absorption }(\,C_0\lt C_t\lt C_s\,)\\ (\,C_t-C_0\,),\,(\,C_s-C_0\,)\Rightarrow\textsf{positive}\\\\ \textsf{For desorption }(\,C_s\lt C_t\lt C_0\,)\\ (\,C_t-C_0\,),\,(\,C_s-C_0\,)\Rightarrow\textsf{negative}\\\\ \frac{V}{A}=\frac{d}{6}\\ \textsf{Coefficient of gas transfer},\,k_g=K_g\,\frac{V}{A}\\ (\,C_s-C_0\,)=\textsf{initial supersaturation} }$$

Design of Gravity Aerators
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Single descent through height h}\\ \textsf{Elapsed time},\,t=\sqrt{\frac{2\,h}{g}}\\\\ \textsf{n descents through the same total height h}\\ t=\sqrt{\frac{2\,n\,h}{g}} }$$

Design of Fixed Spray Aerators
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Time of rise of spray},\,t_r=\frac{1}{2}\,t\\\\ \textsf{Driving head},\,h=\frac{g\,t_r^2}{2\,C_v^2\,\sin^2\alpha}\\\\ \textsf{Horizontal carry},\,r=2\,C_v^2\,h\,\sin 2\,\alpha\\ \textsf{Vertical rise},\,h_r=\frac{1}{2}\,g\,t_r^2\\ \alpha=\textsf{inclination of jet}\\ C_v=\textsf{velocity coefficient }(\,\approx 0.95\,) }$$

Chapter - 9

Sedimentation

Settling of Discrete Particles

Newton's Law
$$\displaylines{ d\ge 0.1\textsf{ to }1.0\,mm\\ R=1\textsf{ to }1000\\\\ \textsf{Settling velocity},\,v_s=\sqrt{\frac{4}{3}\,\frac{g}{C_D}\,(\,S_s-1\,)\,d}\\\\ R=0.5\textsf{ to }10^4\Rightarrow C_d=\frac{24}{R}+\frac{3}{\sqrt{R}}+0.34\\ R\gt 10^3\textsf{ to }10^4\Rightarrow C_d=0.4\\ R\lt 0.5\Rightarrow C_d=\frac{24}{R}\\\\ \textsf{Reynolds number},\,R=\frac{\rho\,v_s\,d}{\mu}=\frac{v_s\,d}{\nu} }$$

Stoke's Law
$$\displaylines{ d\lt 0.1\,mm\\ R\lt 1\\\\ v_s=\frac{1}{18}\,\frac{g}{\nu}\,(\,S_s-1\,)\,d^2\\ d=\textsf{diameter of particle in cm}\\ v_s=\textsf{settling velocity in cm/sec}\\\\ \nu\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 9.2}\\ S=1\quad\textsf{(for water)}\\ \textsf{1 centistoke}=0.01\,cm^2/sec\\\\ v_s=418\,(\,S_s-S\,)\,d^2\left(\frac{3\,t+70}{100}\right)\\ t=\textsf{temperature in degree centigrade}\\ d=\textsf{diameter of particle in mm}\\ v_s=\textsf{settling velocity in mm/sec} }$$

Maximum Velocity to Prevent Bed Uplift or Scour
$$\displaylines{ \\ v_d=\sqrt{\frac{8\,\beta\,g}{f}\,(\,S_s-1\,)\,d}\\\\ \beta=\textsf{0.04 for unigranular sand, 0.06 or more for}\\\textsf{non-uniform (interlocking) sticky materials}\\\\ \textsf{For fine, light and flocculent solids},\\ \frac{v_d}{v_s}=\left(\frac{8}{f}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}\\\\ \textsf{For rectangular basin},\\ \frac{v_d}{v_s}=\frac{L}{H} }$$

Rectangular Sedimentaion Basin
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Assume a working depth of 3 m.}\\\\ \textsf{Provide an extra depth of 1 m for sludge}\\\textsf{storage and 0.5 m for free board.}\\\\ \textsf{Surface loading for plain sedimentation}\\ \textsf{Typical design value}\Rightarrow 15\textsf{ - }30\,m^3/m^2/d }$$

Circular Sedimentaiton Basin
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Let the effective depth}=3\,m\\\\ \textsf{Volume},\,V=D^2\,(\,0.011\,D+0.785\,H\,)\\\\ \textsf{Provide an extra depth of 1.5 m for sludge}\\\textsf{storage and free board.} }$$

Plain Sedimentation Basin
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Velocity of settlement of 0.002 cm diameter}\\\textsf{particles at 26°C can be determined by}\\\textsf{assuming an average specific gravity of 2.65.}\\\\ \textsf{Associated surface loading}\\=864000\,v_s\,\,litres/day/m^2\\\\ \textsf{Assume the depth of tank as 3 m.}\\\\ \textsf{Design surface loading}\\=\textsf{52% of surface loading}\\\\ \textsf{Design detention period}=\textsf{Detention period}\\+\,\textsf{120% of detention period}\\\\ v_d\approx 18\,v_s\\\\ \textsf{Provide an extra depth of 1 m for sludge}\\\textsf{storage and 0.5 m for free board.} }$$

Coagulation Sedimentation Tank
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Alum and iron flocs absorb and entrain water}\\\textsf{and reduce the specific gravity to as little}\\\textsf{as 1.002. Diameter of alum and iron flocs,}\\\textsf{d = 0.1 cm. Settling velocity at 26°C,}\\v_s=0.12\,cm/sec.\\\\ \textsf{Associated surface loading}\\=864000\,v_s\,\,litres/day/m^2\\\\ \textsf{Design surface loading}\\=\textsf{52% of surface loading}\\\\ \textsf{Surface area}=\frac{Q}{\textsf{Design surface loading}}\\\\ \textsf{Provide an effective depth of tank}=3\,m\\ \therefore\textsf{Overall depth}=3+1.5=4.5\,m\\\textsf{(1.5 m = 1 m for sludge and}\\\textsf{0.5 m for free board)}\\\\ \textsf{Theoretical detention time}\\ =\frac{\textsf{Effective depth}}{\textsf{Settling velocity}}\\\\ \textsf{Total actual detention time}\\=\textsf{Detention time}+\textsf{120% of detention time}\\\\ \textsf{Actual velocity of flow in the tank}\\ =\frac{\textsf{Length of tank}}{\textsf{Actual detention time}}\\\\ \textsf{max}\,v_d=18\,v_s\\ \textsf{avg}\,v_d\lt\textsf{max}\,v_d\quad\textsf{(OK)}\\\\ \textsf{Design of floc chamber}\\\\ \textsf{Assume that effective depth of floc chamber}\\\textsf{is equal to half the effective depth of the}\\\textsf{settling tank near floc chamber.}\\\\ \textsf{Assume a detention period of 15 minutes for}\\\textsf{floc chamber.}\\\\ \textsf{Total capacity required}=Q\,t\\\\ \textsf{Width of floc chamber}=\textsf{Width of settling tank}\\\\ \textsf{Length of floc chamber}\\=\frac{\textsf{Total capacity}}{\textsf{Width}\times\textsf{Effective depth}} }$$

Note
$$\displaylines{ v_s=\frac{Q}{LB}\quad\quad t_0=\frac{H}{v_s}\\ \textsf{Capacity of tank,}\,V=LBH=Q\times t_0\\\\ \textsf{For a circular sedimentation tank with}\\\textsf{cone-shaped bottom having a slope of}\\\textsf{1 vertical to 12 horizontal},\\\\ V=D^2\,(\,0.011\,D+0.785\,H\,)\\\\ \textsf{Detention time},\,t_0=\frac{V}{Q}\\\\ \textsf{Surface area of tank}=0.785\,D^2\\\\ \textsf{Overflow rate}=\frac{Q}{\textsf{Surface area}}\\\\ \textsf{Length of weir along the periphery}\\\textsf{of the tank}=\pi\,D\\\\ \textsf{Weir loading per day}=\frac{Q}{\textsf{Length of weir}} }$$

Filtration

Note
$$\displaylines{ Q=0.8\,b\,y^{3/2}\\ Q=\textsf{total water received by the trough }\\(litres/min)\\ b=\textsf{width of the trough in cm}\\ y=\textsf{depth of water}\\\\ \textsf{Provide a free board of 5 cm.} }$$

Rose Equation for Head Loss
$$\displaylines{ R=\frac{v_s\,d}{\nu}\\\\ R\lt 1.9\Rightarrow C_D=\frac{24}{R}\\ R\gt 1.9\textsf{ to }500\Rightarrow C_D=\frac{b}{R^n}\\ b=18.5,\,n=0.6\quad\textsf{(Table 9.1)}\\\\ h_f=\frac{1.067\,v_s^2\,L}{g\,\phi_s\,e^4}\sum_{i=1}^n\,C_{Di}\,\frac{x_i}{d_i}\\\\ \phi_s=\textsf{shape factor}\\ e=\textsf{porosity}\\ C_{Di}=\textsf{drag coefficient for }d_i\\ x_i=\textsf{Wt. fraction of particles retained}\\ 1\,micron=0.001\,mm\\ }$$

Carmen-Kozney Equation
$$\displaylines{ h_f=KL\sum_{i=1}^n\,f_{1i}\,\frac{x_i}{d_i}\\\\ KL=\frac{1}{\phi_s}\,\left(\frac{1-e}{e^3}\right)\left(\frac{v_s^2}{g}\right)\,L\\\\ f_1=150\left(\frac{1-e}{R}\right)+1.75\\\\ R=\phi_s\,\frac{v_s\,d}{\nu} }$$

Uniform Bed
$$\displaylines{ \frac{L_e}{L}=\frac{1-e}{1-e_e}\\\\ e_e=\left(\frac{\bar{v}_s}{v_s}\right)^{0.22}\\\\ \bar{v}_s=v_s\,e_e^{4.545}\\\\ h_f=L\,(\,S_s-1\,)\,(\,1-e\,) }$$

Stratified Bed (Non-Uniform)
$$\displaylines{ L_e=L\,(\,1-e\,)\sum_{i=1}^n\,\frac{x_i}{(\,1-e_e\,)_i} }$$
