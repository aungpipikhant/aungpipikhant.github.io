Chapter 5

Ultimate Bearing Capacity of Shallow Foundations

Terzaghi's Ultimate Bearing Capacity Equation
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Continuous Foundation Supported by a Soil}\\\textsf{with a Rigid Base at Shallow Depth}\\\\ q_u=c'\,N_c+q\,N_q+\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma\,B\,N_{\gamma}\\\\ q_u=\textsf{ultimate bearing capacity}\\ c'=\textsf{cohesion of soil}\\\\ \textsf{Mandel and Salencon (1972)}\\ q_u=c'\,N_c^*+q\,N_q^*+\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma\,B\,N_{\gamma}^*\\ N_c^*\,,\,N_q^*\,,\,N_{\gamma}^*=\textsf{modified bearing}\\\textsf{capacity factors (Figures 5.3, 5.4, 5.5)} \\\\ \phi=\textsf{angle of internal friction}\\ \gamma=\textsf{unit weight of soil}\\ B=\textsf{width of foundation}\\ \textsf{Uniform surcharge,}\,q=\gamma\,D_f\\ D_f=\textsf{depth of foundaion} }$$

Factor of Safety
$$\displaylines{ FS\ge 3\\\\ \textsf{Gross Allowable Bearing Capacity}\\ q_{\textsf{all}}=\frac{q_u}{FS}\\\\ \textsf{Gross Allowable Load}\\ Q_{\textsf{all}}=\frac{q_u\, A}{FS}\\\\ \textsf{Net Allowable Bearing Capacity}\\ q_{u\textsf{ (net)}}=q_u-q }$$

Rectangular Foundation on Granular Soil
$$\displaylines{ q_u=q\,N_q^*\,F_{qs}^*+\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma\,B\,N_{\gamma}^*\,F_{\gamma s}^*\\\\ F_{qs}^*\,,\,F_{\gamma s}^*=\textsf{modified shape factors}\\\\ F_{qs}^*=1-m_1\left(\frac{B}{L}\right)\\\\ F_{\gamma s}^*=1-m_2\left(\frac{B}{L}\right)\\\\ m_1\,,\,m_2\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure 5.6} }$$

Square Foundation on Saturated Clay
$$\displaylines{ q_u=5.14\left(1+\frac{0.5\,\frac{B}{H}-0.707}{5.14}\right)\,c_u+q }$$

Foundation on Layered Clay (Reddy and Srinivasan (1967))
$$\displaylines{ \\\textsf{For undrained loading (\(\phi\) = 0 condition),}\\ c_{u(1)}\,,\,c_{u(2)}=\textsf{shear strength of upper and}\\\quad\textsf{lower clay layers}\\\\ q_u=c_{u(1)}\,N_c\,F_{cs}\,F_{cd}+q\\\\ F_{cs}\,,\,F_{cd}\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 4.3 (Page 170)}\\ N_q\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 4.2 (Page 169)}\\\\ \textsf{For layered soils, the value of \(N_c\)}\\\textsf{is not a constant. Use Figure 5.8b.} }$$

Stronger Soil Underlain by Weaker Soil (Meyorhof and Hanna (1978) and Meyerhof (1974))
$$\displaylines{ \\\textsf{Special cases}\Rightarrow\textsf{Page 229}\\ N_q\,,\,N_{\gamma}\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 4.2 (Page 169)}\\ \textsf{Punching shear coefficient},\,K_s\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure 5.10}\\ F_{qs}\,,\,F_{\gamma s}\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 4.3 (Page 170)}\\\\ q_u\le q_t\quad\textsf{(OK)} }$$

Weaker Soil Underlain by Stronger Soil (Meyorhof and Hanna (1978) and Meyerhof (1974))
$$\displaylines{ \\q_2/q_1\gt 1\\ q_1=c_1'\,N_{c(1)}+\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma_1\,B\,N_{\gamma(1)}\\ q_2=c_2'\,N_{c(2)}+\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma_2\,B\,N_{\gamma(2)}\\\\ q_t=c_1'\,N_{c(1)}\,F_{cs(1)}+\gamma_1\,D_f\,N_{q(1)}\,F_{qs(1)}\\+\,\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma_1\,B\,N_{\gamma(1)}\,F_{\gamma s(1)}\\\\ q_b=c_2'\,N_{c(2)}\,F_{cs(2)}+\gamma_2\,D_f\,N_{q(2)}\,F_{qs(2)}\\+\,\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma_2\,B\,N_{\gamma(2)}\,F_{\gamma s(2)}\\\\ q_u=q_t+(\,q_b-q_t\,)\left(\frac{H}{D}\right)^2\ge q_t\\\\ D\approx B\textsf{ for loose sand and clay}\\ D\approx 2B\textsf{ for dense sand}\\\\ \textsf{Vesic (1975)}\\ q_u=c_{u(1)}\,m\,N_c\,F_{cs}\,F_{cd}+q\\ m\Rightarrow\textsf{Table 5.2, 5.3} }$$

Bearing Capacity of Foundations on Top of a Slope (Meyerhof (1957))
$$\displaylines{ \\\textsf{For purely granular soil, \(c'\) = 0},\\ q_u=\frac{1}{2}\,\gamma\,B\,N_{\gamma q}\\ N_{\gamma q}\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure 5.20}\\\\ \textsf{For purely cohesive soil, \(\phi\) = 0}\\ \textsf{(the undrained condition)},\\ q_u=c_u\,\,N_{cq}\\ B\lt H\Rightarrow N_s=0\\ B\ge H\Rightarrow N_s=\frac{\gamma\,H}{c_u}\\ N_{cq}\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure 5.21} }$$

Chapter 8

Mat Foundations

Rectangular Combined Footing
$$\displaylines{ A=\frac{Q_1+Q_2}{q_{\textsf{net(all)}}}\\\\ X=\frac{Q_2\,L_3}{Q_1+Q_2}\\\\ \textsf{For a uniform distribution of soil pressure}\\\textsf{under the foundation, the resultant of the}\\\textsf{column loads should pass through the}\\\textsf{centroid of the foundation.}\\ L=2\,(\,L_2+X\,)\\ L_1=L-L_2-L_3\\\\ B=\frac{A}{L} }$$

Trapezoidal Combined Footing
$$\displaylines{ A=\frac{Q_1+Q_2}{q_{\textsf{net(all)}}}\\\\ A=\frac{B_1+B_2}{2}\,L\\\\ X=\frac{Q_2\,L_3}{Q_1+Q_2}\\\\ X+L_2=\left(\frac{B_1+2\,B_2}{B_1+B_2}\right)\frac{L}{3}\\\\ \frac{L}{3}\lt X+L_2\lt\frac{L}{2} }$$

Bearing Capacity of Mat Foundation
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Constructed over saturated clay},\\ q_{\textsf{net(u)}}=5.14\,c_u\left(1+\frac{0.195\, B}{L}\right)\left(1+0.4\frac{D_f}{B}\right)\\\\ \textsf{Constructed over granular soil},\\ q_{\textsf{net(all)}}=0.25\,N_{60}\left[1+0.33\left(\frac{D_f}{B}\right)\right]S_e\\\\ N_{60}=\textsf{standard penetration resistence}\\ S_e=\textsf{settlement} }$$

Compensated Foundation
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{For saturated clays},\\ FS=\frac{5.14\,c_u\left(1+\frac{0.195\, B}{L}\right)\left(1+0.4\frac{D_f}{B}\right)}{\frac{Q}{A}-\gamma\,D_f} }$$

Primary Consolidation Settlement
$$\displaylines{ q=\frac{Q}{A}-\gamma D_f\\\\ \textsf{For }I_{a(H_1)}\,,\\ m_2=\frac{B}{H_1}\quad\quad n_2=\frac{L}{H_1}\\ \textsf{Use Figure (6.11)}\\\\ \textsf{For }I_{a(H_2)}\,,\\ m_2=\frac{B}{H_2}\quad\quad n_2=\frac{L}{H_2}\\ \textsf{Use Figure (6.11)}\\\\ \Delta \sigma'_{\textsf{av}(H_2/H_1)}=q_0\left[\,\frac{H_2\,I_{a(H_2)}-H_1\,I_{a(H_1)}}{H_2-H_1}\,\right]\\\\ S_{c\,(p)}=\frac{C_c\,H_c}{1+e_0}\log \left(\,\frac{\sigma_0'+\Delta\sigma'_{\textsf{av}}}{\sigma_0'}\,\right) }$$

Structural Design of Mat Foundations
$$\displaylines{ Q=Q_1+Q_2+Q_3\,+...\\\\ A=B\times L\\\\ I_x=\frac{1}{12}BL^3\\\\ I_y=\frac{1}{12}LB^3\\\\ M_y=Q\,e_x\\\\ e_x=x'-\frac{B}{2}\\\\ x'=\frac{Q_1\,x_1'+Q_2\,x_2'+Q_3\,x_3'+...}{Q}\\\\ y'=\frac{Q_1\,y_1'+Q_2\,y_2'+Q_3\,y_3'+...}{Q}\\\\ e_y=y'-\frac{L}{2}\\\\ M_x=Q\,e_y\\\\ q=\frac{Q}{A}\pm\frac{M_y\,x}{I_y}\pm\frac{M_x\,y}{I_x} }$$

Pile Foundations

Point Bearing Capacity, \(Q_p\)
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Meyerhof's Method}\\\\ Q_p=A_p\,q'N_q^*\le A_p\,q_l\\\\ q'=\gamma L\\\\ q_l=0.5\,p_a\,N_q^*\tan\phi'\\\\ N_q^*\Rightarrow\textsf{Table (9.5)}\\\\\\ \textsf{Vesic's Method}\\\\ Q_p=A_p\,\bar{\sigma}_0'\,N_{\sigma}^*\\\\ \bar{\sigma}_0'=\left(\frac{1+2K_0}{3}\right)\,q'\\\\ K_0=1-\sin\phi'\\\\ \frac{E_s}{p_a}=m\\ m=250\quad\textsf{(medium dense soil)}\\\\ \mu_s=0.1+0.3\left(\frac{\phi'-25}{20}\right)\\\\ \Delta=0.005\left(1-\frac{\phi'-25}{20}\right)\frac{q'}{p_a}\\\\ I_r=\frac{E_s}{2(1+\mu_s)\,q'\tan\phi'}\\\\ I_{rr}=\frac{I_r}{1+I_r\,\Delta}\\\\N_{\sigma}^*\Rightarrow\textsf{Table (9.7)}\\\\ \textsf{Coyle and Castello's Method}\\\\ Q_p=q'N_q^*A_p\\ N_q^*\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure (9.15)} }$$

Frictional Resistance, \(Q_s\)
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Using equations (9.41) and (9.42)}\\ L'=15D\\ f=K\sigma_0'\tan\delta'\\ Q_s=f_{\textsf{av}}\,pL'\\\\ \textsf{Using equatioin (9.44)}\\ Q_s=K\,\bar{\sigma}_0'\tan(0.8\phi')\,pL\\ K\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure (9.17)}\\\\ Q_{\textsf{all}}=\frac{Q_p+Q_s}{FS} }$$

Frictional (Skin) Resistance in Clay
$$\displaylines{ \alpha\textsf{ Method}\\\\ Q_s=\sum\alpha\, C_u \,p\,\Delta L\\ p=\pi D\\ \alpha\Rightarrow\textsf{Table (9.10)}\\\\ \lambda\textsf{ Method}\\\\ Q_s=p\,L\,f_{\textsf{av}}\\\\ \bar{\sigma}_0'=\frac{A_1+A_2+A_3}{L}\\\\ c_u=\frac{c_{u(1)}\,L_1+c_{u(2)}\,L_2+c_{u(3)}\,L_3}{L}\\\\ \lambda\Rightarrow\textsf{Table (9.9)}\\ f_{\textsf{av}}=\lambda\,(\bar{\sigma}_0'+2\,c_u)\\\\ \beta\textsf{ Method}\\\\ Q_s=\sum f\,p\,\Delta L\\\\ \textsf{For normally consolidated clay,}\\ f=(1-\sin\phi_R')\tan\phi_R'\,\sigma_0'\\\\ \textsf{For overconsolidated clay,}\\ f=(1-\sin\phi_R')\tan\phi_R'\,\sqrt{OCR}\,\,\sigma_0'\\\\ Q_u=Q_p+Q_s\\ Q_{\textsf{all}}=\frac{Q_u}{FS} }$$

Group Piles
$$\displaylines{ \sum Q_u=n_1n_2\left[\,9A_p\,c_{u(p)}+\sum \alpha\, p\,c_u\,\Delta L\,\right]\\\\ \alpha\Rightarrow\textsf{Table (9.10)}\\ N_c^*\Rightarrow\textsf{Figure (9.48)}\\\\ \sum Q_u\\=L_g\,B_g\,c_{u(p)}\,N_c^*+\sum 2\,(L_g+B_g)\,c_u\,\Delta L\\\\ \textsf{(smaller control)}\\\\ \sum Q_{\textsf{all}}=\frac{\sum Q_u}{FS} }$$

Consolidation Settlement of Group Piles
