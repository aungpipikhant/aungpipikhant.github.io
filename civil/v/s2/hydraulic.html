Design of Channels

Kennedy's Theory
$$\displaylines{ V=0.55\,m\,D^{0.64}\\\\ A=\frac{Q}{V}\\\\ A=BD+0.5D^2\\\\ P=B+D\sqrt{5}\\\\ R=\frac{A}{P}\\\\ V=\frac{\frac{1}{N}+(\,23+\frac{0.00155}{S}\,)}{1+(\,23+\frac{0.00155}{S}\,)\left(\frac{N}{\sqrt{R}}\right)}\,\sqrt{RS} }$$

Lacey's Theory
$$\displaylines{ f=1.76\,\sqrt{m_r}\\\\ V=\left[\,\frac{Qf^2}{140}\,\right]^{\frac{1}{6}}\\\\ P=4.75\,\sqrt{Q}\\\\ A=BD+0.5D^2\\\\ P=B+D\sqrt{5}\\\\ S=\frac{f^{5/3}}{3340\,Q^{1/6}}\\\\ R=\frac{A}{P}\quad\quad R=\frac{5}{2}\frac{V^2}{f} }$$

Meyer-Peter's Equations
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Quantity of bed load transported}=Q_s\\ \textsf{Regime perimeter, }P=4.75\,\sqrt{Q}\\ \textsf{Rate of bed load transported, }q_s=\frac{Q_s}{P}\\\\ N'=\frac{(\,k_s\,)^{\frac{1}{6}}}{24}\\\\ \textsf{Bed shear, }\tau_b=\omega DS\\\\ \textsf{Critical shear stress, }\tau_c=0.047(\,\omega_s-\omega\,)d\\\\ q_s=47500\,\left[\,\tau_b\left(\frac{N'}{N}\right)^{\frac{3}{2}}-\tau_c\,\right]^\frac{3}{2}\\\\ \textsf{Bed load, }Q_s=q_s\,B }$$

Cross Regulator
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Crest level}=\textsf{U/s bed level}\\ \textsf{Neglect }h_a.\\ \textsf{Take }C_{d_1}=0.577,\,C_{d_2}=0.8\\ h=\textsf{U/s F.S.L}-\textsf{D/s F.S.L}\\ d=\textsf{D/s F.S.L}-\textsf{Crest level}\\\\ Q=\frac{2}{3}\,C_{d_1}\,\sqrt{2g}\,L\,[\,(h+h_a)^{\frac{3}{2}}-h_a^{\frac{3}{2}}\,]\\+\,C_{d_2}\,L\,d\times\sqrt{2g\,(h+h_a)}\\\\ \textsf{Depth of U/s cutoff, }d_1=\frac{D}{3}+0.6\\ \textsf{Depth of D/s cutoff, }d_2=\frac{D}{2}+0.6\\\\ \textsf{Maximum static head, }\\ H=\textsf{U/s F.S.L}-\textsf{D/s bed level}\\\\ \textsf{Exit gradient, }G_E=\frac{H}{d_2}\times\frac{1}{\pi\sqrt{\lambda}}\\\\ \lambda=\frac{1+\sqrt{1+\alpha^2}}{2}\\\\ \textsf{Total length of impervious floor, }b=\alpha\, d_2\\\\ \textsf{Length of D/s Impervious Floor}\\ q=\frac{Q}{L}\quad\quad H_L=h\\\\ E_{f_2}\Rightarrow\textsf{Blench curve}\\ \textsf{D/s floor level}=\textsf{D/s T.E.L}-E_{f_2}\\ E_{f_1}=E_{f_2}+H_L\\\\ y_1,\,y_2\Rightarrow\textsf{Montague's curve}\\\\ \textsf{Check}\\ E_{f_1}=y_1+\left[\,\frac{q}{y_1}\,\right]^2\times\frac{1}{2g}\\ E_{f_2}=y_2+\left[\,\frac{q}{y_2}\,\right]^2\times\frac{1}{2g}\\\\ \textsf{Length of D/s floor, }L_d=5\,(y_2-y_1)\\ \textsf{Minimum length of D/s floor}=\frac{2}{3}\,b }$$
