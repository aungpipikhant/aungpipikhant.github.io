Reservior Planning

Trapezoidal Formula
$$\displaylines{ V=\frac{h}{2}\,[\,A_1+2\,A_2+2\,A_3\,+\,...\\+\,2\,A_{n-1}+A_n\,] }$$

Cone Formula
$$\displaylines{ \Delta\,V_1=\frac{h}{3}\,(\,A_1+A_2+\sqrt{A_1\,A_2}\,)\\\\ V=\Delta\,V_1+\Delta\,V_2+\Delta\,V_3\,+\,...\,=\sum\,\Delta\,V }$$

Prismoidal Formula
$$\displaylines{ V=\frac{h}{3}\,[\,(\,A_1+A_n\,)\\+\,4\,(\,A_2+A_4+A_6\,+\,...\,)\\+\,2\,(\,A_3+A_5\,+\,...\,)\,]\\\\ \textsf{The prismoidal formula is applicable only}\\\textsf{when there are odd numbers of areas}\\\textsf{(i.e. n should be an odd number).}\\\\ n=\textsf{even}\Rightarrow V=V_1+V_2 }$$

Analytical Method
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Adjusted inflow}=\textsf{Stream inflow}\\+\,\textsf{Precipitation}-\textsf{Evaporation}\\-\,\textsf{Downstream Discharge}\\\\ \textsf{Storage required}=\textsf{Adjusted inflow}-\textsf{Demand}\\\\ \textsf{1 cumec-month}=1\times 60\times 60\times 24\times 30\\=259.2\,ha\textsf{-}m }$$

Notes
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Concrete unit weight},\,\omega_c=24\,kN/m^3\\ \textsf{Water unit weight},\,\omega=9.81\,kN/m^3\,(10\,kN/m^3)\\\\ \textsf{Water pressure (u/s)},\\ P_H=\frac{1}{2}\times\omega\,H\times H=\frac{\omega\,H^2}{2}\\\\ \textsf{Tail water pressure (d/s)},\\ P_{H'}=\frac{\omega\,(\,H'\,)^2}{2}\\\\ p=\omega\,H\quad\quad p'=\omega\,H' }$$

Earthquake Forces on Water
$$\displaylines{ h\gt\frac{H}{2}\Rightarrow\textsf{Consider u/s face vertical.}\\ C_m=0.735\,(\,1-\frac{\phi}{90°}\,)\\ p_e=C\,\alpha_h\,\omega\,H\\ P_e=0.726\,(\,p_e\,H\,)\\ M_e=0.299\,(\,p_e\,H^2\,) }$$

Tension Failure
$$\displaylines{ \bar{x}=\frac{\sum\,M}{\sum\,V}\\ e=\frac{B}{2}-\bar{x}\\ e\lt\frac{B}{6}\Rightarrow\textsf{There is no tension.}\\ e\gt\frac{B}{6}\Rightarrow\textsf{Tension is developed.} }$$

Vertical Stresses at the Base of the Dam
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{Reservior Full}\\ \textsf{At toe (d/s) edge},\,f_{yd}=\frac{\sum\,V}{B}\,(\,1+\frac{6\,e}{B}\,)\\ \textsf{At heel (u/s) edge},\,f_{yu}=\frac{\sum\,V}{B}\,(\,1-\frac{6\,e}{B}\,)\\\\ \textsf{Reservior Empty}\\ \textsf{At toe (d/s) edge},\,f_{yd}=\frac{\sum\,V}{B}\,(\,1-\frac{6\,e}{B}\,)\\ \textsf{At heel (u/s) edge},\,f_{yu}=\frac{\sum\,V}{B}\,(\,1+\frac{6\,e}{B}\,)\\\\ \textsf{\(f_{yd}\) and \(f_{yu}\)}\\ \textsf{(+) Compression}\\ \textsf{(-) Tension} }$$

Principal and Shear Stresses
$$\displaylines{ \textsf{At toe (d/s)}\\ \sigma_d=f_{yd}\,\sec^2\phi_d-(\,p'-p_e'\,)\,\tan^2\phi_d\\ \tau_d=[\,f_{yd}-(\,p'-p_e'\,)\,]\,\tan\phi_d\\ \textsf{No tail water}\Rightarrow p'=0,\,p_e'=0\\ \sigma_d=f_{yd}\,\sec^2\phi_d\\ \tau_d=f_{yd}\,\tan\phi_d\\\\ \textsf{At heel (u/s)}\\ \sigma_u=f_{yu}\,\sec^2\phi_u-(\,p+p_e\,)\,\tan^2\phi_u\\ \tau_u=-[\,f_{yu}-(\,p+p_e\,)\,]\,\tan\phi_u\\ \textsf{Reservior empty}\Rightarrow p=0,\,p_e=0\\ \sigma_u=f_{yu}\,\sec^2\phi_u\\ \tau_u=-f_{yu}\,\tan\phi_u\\\\ \textsf{\(\sigma_d\) and \(\sigma_u\)}\\ \textsf{(+) Compression}\\ \textsf{(-) Tension}\\\\ \textsf{\(\tau_d\) and \(\tau_u\)}\\ \textsf{(+) Towards upstream}\\ \textsf{(-) Towards downstream} }$$

Failure Due to Overturning
$$\displaylines{ F_O=\frac{\sum\,M_R}{\sum\,M_O}\gt 1.5\,\textsf{(safe)} }$$

Failure Due to Sliding

Approximate Method for Checking the Stability of Earth Dams

Stability of U/s Slope
$$\displaylines{ H_u=\frac{\omega_s\,H^2}{2}\,\tan^2\left[\,45°-\frac{\phi}{2}\,\right]+\frac{\omega\,h_1^2}{2}\\\\ \tau_a=\frac{H_u}{b_u}\\\\ R_u=W_u'\,\tan\phi+c\,b_u\\\\ \textsf{Factor of safety},\,F_s=\frac{R_u}{H_u}\gt 2\\\\ \tau_{\textsf{max}}=2\,\tau_a\\\\ \textsf{Maximum shear stress occurs at a point}\\\textsf{of distance of 0.4 }b_u.\\ s_{\textsf{max}}=\omega_s'\,h\,\tan\phi+c\\\\ \textsf{Factor of safety against maximum shear},\\ F_s'=\frac{s_{\textsf{max}}}{\tau_{\textsf{max}}}\gt 1.5\\\\ H_u=\textsf{horizontal force acting on U/s of dam}\\ \omega_s=\textsf{saturated unit weight of soil in U/s}\\ \omega=\textsf{unit weight of water}\\ \omega_s'=\textsf{submerged unit weight of soil}\\ H=\textsf{dam height}\\ \phi=\textsf{angle of internal friction of soil}\\ h_1=\textsf{vertical distance from phreatic line to}\\\textsf{base of dam}\\ \tau_a=\textsf{average shear stress at base}\\ b_u=\textsf{width of base of U/s of dam}\\ R_u=\textsf{resisting force against horizontal}\\\textsf{shear stress}\\ W_u'=\textsf{total effective weight of U/s of dam}\\ h=\textsf{height at 0.4 }b_u }$$

Stability of D/s Slope
$$\displaylines{ H_d=\frac{\omega_s\,H^2}{2}\,\tan^2\left[\,45°-\frac{\phi}{2}\,\right]+\frac{\omega\,h_1^2}{2}\\\\ \tau_a=\frac{H_d}{b_d}\\\\ R_d=W_d\,\tan\phi+c\,b_d\\\\ \textsf{Factor of safety},\,F_s=\frac{R_d}{H_d}\gt 2\\\\ \tau_{\textsf{max}}=2\,\tau_a\\\\ \textsf{Maximum shear stress occurs at a point}\\\textsf{of distance of 0.4 }b_d.\\ s_{\textsf{max}}=\omega_s\,h\,\tan\phi+c\\\\ \textsf{Factor of safety against maximum shear},\\ F_s'=\frac{s_{\textsf{max}}}{\tau_{\textsf{max}}}\gt 1.5\\\\ H_d=\textsf{horizontal force acting on D/s of dam}\\ \omega_s=\textsf{weighted unit weight of soil in D/s}\\ \omega=\textsf{unit weight of water}\\ H=\textsf{dam height}\\ \phi=\textsf{angle of internal friction of soil}\\ h_1=\textsf{vertical distance from phreatic line to}\\\textsf{base of dam}\\ \tau_a=\textsf{average shear stress at base}\\ b_d=\textsf{width of base of D/s of dam}\\ R_d=\textsf{resisting force against sliding}\\ W_d=\textsf{total effective weight of D/s of dam}\\ h=\textsf{height at 0.4 }b_d }$$
