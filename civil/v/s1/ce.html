Determining Pile Load Capacity
$$\displaylines{ R=\left(\,\frac{2\,E}{S+0.1}\,\right)\left(\,\frac{W_r+K\,W_p}{W_r+W_p}\,\right)\\\\ R=\textsf{safe load (lb)}\\ S=\textsf{average penetration per blow,}\\\textsf{last six blows (in)}\\ E=\textsf{energy of hammer (ft-lb)}\\\\ K=\textsf{coefficient of restitution}\\ \textsf{0.2 for piles weighing 50 lb/ft or less}\\ \textsf{0.4 for piles weighing 50 to 100 lb/ft}\\ \textsf{0.6 for piles weighing over 100 lb/ft}\\\\ W_r=\textsf{weight of hammer ram (lb)}\\ W_p=\textsf{weight of pile, including driving}\\\textsf{appurtenances (lb)} }$$

Safe Load Capacity of Bulb Piles
$$\displaylines{ L=\frac{W\times H\times B\times V^{2/3}}{K}\\\\ L=\textsf{safe load capacity (tons)}\\ W=\textsf{weight of hammar (tons)}\\ H=\textsf{height of drop (ft)}\\ B=\textsf{number of blows per cubic foot}\\ V=\textsf{uncompacted volume of concrete (cu ft)}\\ K=\textsf{dimensionless constant} }$$
